How do glow worms light themselves up?

Glow worms are something we all see. They are also known as Fireflies. They are lit by small lights that we observe at night. How do they actually shine so beautifully on their own? Now let's see why they actually light up.

There are over 2000 species of these glowing insects that we see. Lights come in a variety of colors such as yellow, green, and orange. However, the chemical reaction that occurs in living organisms is not known as bioluminescence. Not only this, with the help of sea urchins and jellyfish you can do wonders.

However, these fireflies have an organ called the lantern, which is the cause of the lights. These organs contain a substance called luciferin, as well as an enzyme called luciferase. When the glow worm breathes in, it reaches the lantern, an organ that contains some oxygen, and combines with lysine rhin and luciferase to form a chemical reaction.

But it's not like the lights coming from the lights that light up our house. In light bulbs, 90% of the energy is released in the form of heat and the remaining 10% in the form of light. But glow worms are released in 100% lys forms. That is why it is not called Cold Light.
